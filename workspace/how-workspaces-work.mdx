# **How Workspaces Work in Lunacal**

A **workspace** is your main place for scheduling in Lunacal.

Think of it like a container for:

- your booking links
- your event types
- your booking pages
- your routing forms

You can use a workspace as **one person** or as a **team**.

---

## **The simple idea**

A workspace helps Lunacal decide **where bookings should go**.

- If you work alone, it can behave like your personal scheduling home.
- If you work with others, it can also run shared team booking flows.
- The same workspace screen can show both personal and team options.

So yes, workspaces are for teams **and** individuals.

---

## **Personal bookings vs team bookings**

## **Personal bookings**

- Booking is for one person.
- Your attendee books directly with you.
- Link usually points to your personal page/event.

## **Team bookings**

- Booking starts from a team page or team event.
- Lunacal can involve multiple people depending on the event setup.
- Team events can be:
  - one selected team member
  - any available team member
  - multiple team members together (for group/team-style meetings)

---

## **How event types, booking pages, and routing forms connect**

## **Event types**

These are the meeting options you create (example: Intro Call, Demo, Support Session).

## **Booking pages**

These are the public pages attendees open to pick a time.

- Personal booking pages show your personal events.
- Team booking pages can show team events and/or team members.

## **Routing forms**

These ask questions first, then send people to the right booking option.

- A routing form can send someone to:
  - an event booking page, or
  - another link/page/message
- This helps direct the right person to the right calendar without manual triage.

---

## **What happens when someone books**

1. Attendee opens a booking link (or finishes a routing form).
2. Lunacal shows available times.
3. When they confirm, Lunacal creates the booking and sends confirmations.
4. The booking is assigned based on your event setup:
   - personal event: goes to that person
   - team event: uses team assignment rules
   - managed one-to-one setup: goes to one assigned member (including “any available” behavior)

For “any available” in managed one-to-one flows, Lunacal selects from assigned members automatically.

---

## **What your attendees see**

Your attendees see a **clean booking experience**:

- they see a booking page
- they choose a time
- they get confirmation

They do **not** see workspace complexity (internal team/workspace setup screens, assignment settings, or management structure).

Even if your backend setup is complex, attendee experience stays simple.

---

## **Why you see team links even when you work alone**

This is expected.

Lunacal uses one workspace system that supports both solo and team use.\
So the interface can still show team-style areas (like team booking pages/links), even if you are the only person using it right now.

It does not mean you must run a team.

---

## **Why some settings are locked (managed events)**

If an event is a **managed** team event, some settings can be locked for assigned members.

That means:

- an admin/owner controls core settings at the main managed event level
- assigned members can only change fields that were allowed

This keeps team booking behavior consistent.

---

## **Real examples**

## **Example 1: Solo consultant**

You use one workspace for your own meetings.

- Create personal event types (like “30-min Consultation”).
- Share your booking link.
- Optional: use a routing form to qualify leads before sending them to your event.

You may still see team/workspace UI options, but your attendee just sees your booking page.

## **Example 2: Sales \+ Support team**

Your workspace has shared team scheduling.

- Sales team has events for demos.
- Support team has events for onboarding/help calls.
- A routing form asks “What do you need?” and routes to the correct booking flow.
- For managed one-to-one events, attendees can choose a specific member or “any available,” and Lunacal assigns accordingly.

Attendees only see the booking flow, not the internal setup rules.

---

## **Quick takeaway**

A workspace is not “team-only.”\
It is the scheduling home that powers both:

- **personal booking flows**, and
- **team booking flows**

The UI can show team capabilities even for individual users, but attendees always get a simple booking experience.
