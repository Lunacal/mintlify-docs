## What is a Workspace?

A workspace is simply **your home base in lunacal**. It's where all your scheduling lives — your event types, your bookings, your settings.

Every Lunacal account starts with a **Personal Workspace**. This is yours. You don't need a team to use it. You don't need to invite anyone. If you're a solo consultant, freelancer, or individual professional, your Personal Workspace is all you need.

If you work with others — a sales team, a support team, a group practice — you can also create a **Team Workspace**. A team workspace lets multiple people share event types, booking pages, and scheduling rules.

**The short version:** Workspace = where your scheduling stuff lives. Personal workspace for you. Team workspace for groups.

**Personal Bookings vs. Team Bookings**

**Personal Bookings**

When you create an event type in your Personal Workspace, it belongs to you alone.

- **Your link:** lunacal.ai/yourname/event-name
- **Who gets booked:** You. Only you.
- **Your calendar:** Only your availability matters.
- **You control everything:** Duration, location, price, custom questions — it's all yours to set.

This is the simplest setup. One person, one calendar, one link.

**Team Bookings**

When you create an event type inside a Team Workspace, it can involve multiple people. There are three ways team bookings work:

- **Round Robin** — Meetings are distributed one-by-one across your team members. Lunacal picks whoever was booked least recently, so the load stays balanced. If you want, you can let the person booking choose a specific team member from a dropdown.
- **Collective** — The meeting only gets booked when _everyone_ on the team is free at the same time. Useful for group interviews, panel calls, or any meeting where the whole team needs to be present.
- **One to One Event** **( Managed Events )** — An admin/owner creates one event type and pushes it out to selected team members. Each member gets their own personal version of that event with the same settings. This is how you keep things consistent across a team without making each person set up their own event from scratch. _(More on this below.)_

**How Event Types, Booking Pages, and Routing Forms Fit Together**

Think of it like this:

- **Event types** are the meetings you offer (e.g., "30-min Consultation," "Product Demo").
- **Booking pages** are where people go to pick a time and book with you.
- **Routing forms** are optional intake forms that ask questions first, then send the person to the right event type based on their answers.

They connect like a funnel:

**Routing Form** (optional) → asks questions → sends to the right **Booking Page** → person picks a time → **Booking confirmed**

You don't need routing forms for simple setups. But if you offer multiple services or need to qualify leads before they book, routing forms save everyone time.

**What Happens When Someone Books**

Here's what the process looks like, start to finish:

1. The person visits your booking link (or fills out a routing form that sends them there).
2. They see a calendar with your available dates and times.
3. They pick a time slot.
4. They fill in their details — name, email, and any custom questions you've added.
5. For paid events, they enter payment and can apply a coupon code if you've set one up.
6. They click **Confirm** (or **Pay and Book** for paid events).
7. They immediately see a confirmation page with the meeting details, location, and options to add it to their calendar.
8. They get a confirmation email with everything they need — time, location, a calendar file to import, and links to reschedule or cancel if needed.

For round-robin team events, Lunacal automatically assigns the team member behind the scenes. For collective events, all team members are added to the meeting.

**Why You See Team Features (Even If You're Solo)**

This is one of the most common sources of confusion. Here's what's going on:

**"Why do I see a Teams link in my sidebar?"**

lunacal shows the Teams section to everyone — even solo users. It's there so you can discover team features when you need them, and so you can see and accept team invitations if someone invites you. If you're not using teams, you can simply ignore it.

**"Why does the workspace dropdown show 'Your Teams'?"**

Your workspace selector always shows two sections: **Personal Workspace** at the top, and **Your Teams** below it. If you haven't created or joined any teams, the team section just shows an option to create one. Your Personal Workspace is always there and always works independently.

**"Why are some of my event settings locked or grayed out?"**

If a team admin/owner created a **managed event** and assigned it to you, some settings are controlled by the admin/owner — not you. This is by design. It keeps things consistent across the team. You'll see a note like _"This event type is managed by \[Team Name\]"_ on those events.

You can still use the event and get booked, but the admin controls things like duration, title, and custom fields. If you need something changed, ask your team admin.

**"Why does it feel like everything is built for teams?"**

Lunacal uses the same workspace structure for individuals and teams. Your Personal Workspace uses the same tools and interface — it's not a limited version. The team features are additions on top, not replacements. The interface may show team-related options, but they don't affect your personal scheduling unless you actively set up a team.

**Managed Events: What They Are and Why Settings Are Locked**

Managed events solve a real problem: when a team admin needs 10 people to offer the same meeting type with the same settings.

Here's how they work:

1. A team admin creates a managed event type (e.g., "Initial Consultation — 30 min").
2. The admin assigns it to specific team members — or to the entire team automatically, including future members.
3. Each assigned member gets their own copy of that event on their personal booking page.
4. The link for each member looks like: lunacal.ai/membername/event-name
5. The admin controls the settings. Members can't change the title, duration, or other locked fields.

**If you're a team member with a managed event:** You'll see it in your event types list with a note that it's managed. You can get booked through it normally. You just can't edit the settings the admin has locked down.

**If you're an admin:** You create it once, assign it, and any changes you make automatically apply to everyone's copy.

**Routing Forms: Sending People to the Right Place**

Routing forms are useful when you need to ask a few questions before someone books.

Here's what the person sees:

1. A simple form with your questions (text fields, dropdowns, radio buttons, etc.).
2. They fill it in and hit submit.
3. Based on their answers, Lunacal automatically sends them to the right booking page — or shows them a custom message, or redirects them to an external link.

Their form answers can pre-fill the booking form, so they don't have to type the same info twice.

Routing forms can belong to your Personal Workspace or to a Team Workspace.

**Real-World Examples**

**Example 1: Sarah, a Solo Career Coach**

Sarah uses Lunacal on her own. She has:

- A Personal Workspace with her name and branding
- Two event types: "Discovery Call (15 min)" and "Full Coaching Session (60 min)"
- A booking page at lunacal.ai/sarah-coach

Her clients visit her link, pick an event, choose a time, and book. Sarah gets notified, the meeting goes on her calendar. Done.

She sees "Teams" in her sidebar but ignores it — it doesn't affect anything.

**Example 2: Greenline Sales Team**

Greenline has a 5-person sales team. Their team admin, Jake, sets things up:

- He creates a **Team Workspace** called "Greenline Sales"
- He creates a **managed event** called "Product Demo (30 min)" and assigns it to all 5 reps
- Each rep gets their own bookable link: lunacal.ai/jake/product-demo, lunacal.ai/maria/product-demo, etc.
- He also creates a **round-robin event** called "Talk to Sales" on the team booking page — leads who don't have a specific rep get automatically assigned to whoever was booked least recently
- He sets up a **routing form** on the website that asks "Are you a new or existing customer?" — new customers go to the round-robin "Talk to Sales" event, existing customers go to their assigned rep's link

The reps see the managed event in their list but can't change the settings Jake configured. Their personal event types (like "1:1 Check-in") remain fully under their control in their own Personal Workspaces.

Customers visiting the booking page see a clean, simple experience. They have no idea about workspaces, managed events, or round-robin logic. They just pick a time and book.

**Quick Reference**

| **Concept** | **What It Means** |
| :-- | :-- |
| Personal Workspace | Your own scheduling home base. Always there. |
| Team Workspace | A shared space for a group to manage scheduling together. |
| Event Type | A meeting you offer (e.g., "30-min Call"). |
| Booking Page | The public page where someone picks a time and books. |
| Routing Form | An optional questionnaire that directs people to the right event. |
| Managed Event | An event type created by an admin and pushed to team members. Settings are locked. |
| Round Robin | Automatically distributes bookings across team members evenly. |
| Collective | Books only when all team members are available at the same time. |
