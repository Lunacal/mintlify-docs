---
title: "Limits and Buffer"
description: "Set booking limits and buffer times to control how often people can book you and to give yourself prep/transition time. You’ll find these controls under “Event Types” → “Booking Limits” inside “Limits and Buffer”."
mode: "wide"
---

## Step 1

On “Event Types”, select the event type you want to edit.

<Frame>
  ![](/images/limits_and_buffer/1.webp)
</Frame>

## Step 2

Click “Booking Limits”.

<Frame>
  ![](/images/limits_and_buffer/2.webp)
</Frame>

## Step 3

In “Limits and Buffer”, set a buffer before and after meetings.\
Example: Add a 10-minute buffer before a demo to review notes and load the deck.

<Frame>
  ![](/images/limits_and_buffer/3.webp)
</Frame>

## Step 4

Set a buffer after meetings to avoid back-to-back calls.\
Example: Add 15–20 minutes after a sales call to write follow-up points and reset.

<Frame>
  ![](/images/limits_and_buffer/4.webp)
</Frame>

## Step 5

In the advance booking notice section, you can define the lead time required before bookings can be made, This ensures that bookings cannot be made immediately, such as within an hour or two.

<Frame>
  ![](/images/limits_and_buffer/5.webp)
</Frame>

## Step 6

Additionally, you can limit the booking frequency, for instance, restricting to one booking per day, month, or year.

<Frame>
  ![](/images/limits_and_buffer/6.webp)
</Frame>

## Step 7

Set the “Advance Booking Limit” (how far into the future people can see and book).\
Example: Show only the next 30 days so your calendar doesn’t get locked months ahead.

<Frame>
  ![](/images/limits_and_buffer/7.webp)
</Frame>

## Step 8

Once all settings are in place, click the "Save" button to apply your limits and buffer settings.

<Frame>
  ![](/images/limits_and_buffer/8.webp)
</Frame>
