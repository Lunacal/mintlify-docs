# Streamlining Meeting Management

> Efficiently manage your meetings using the Bookings tab, enabling you to oversee upcoming, past, unconfirmed, and cancelled meetings. This feature provides detailed visibility and control over your scheduling, ensuring seamless organization and adjustments when necessary.

### Step 1

Begin by clicking on the "Bookings" tab located in the left menu to access the full suite of scheduling features.

<Frame><img src="/images/bookingsTab/image1.jpg" /></Frame>

### Step 2

On this page, you will find a comprehensive list of all upcoming, unconfirmed, past, and cancelled meetings. The client's name and event details are displayed here. Clicking on a specific event reveals further meeting details.

<Frame><img src="/images/bookingsTab/image2.jpg" /></Frame>

### Step 3

Here, you can view detailed information including the event name, date and time, attendees, and location. Additionally, you have the option to reschedule or cancel the meeting from this section.

<Frame><img src="/images/bookingsTab/image3.jpg" /></Frame>

### Step 4

If necessary, you can reschedule the meeting directly from this interface.

<Frame><img src="/images/bookingsTab/image4.jpg" /></Frame>

### Step 5

Should you be unavailable at the scheduled time, you can request a reschedule with the booker. You also have the ability to edit the meeting location.

<Frame><img src="/images/bookingsTab/image5.jpg" /></Frame>

### Step 6

Select your preferred method of communication, such as a phone number, organization phone number, or Google Meet, as per your requirements.

<Frame><img src="/images/bookingsTab/image6.jpg" /></Frame>

### Step 7

In the unconfirmed section, you can view bookings that are pending. This includes bookings that have been paid for but not confirmed by the booker.

<Frame><img src="/images/bookingsTab/image7.jpg" /></Frame>

### Step 8

The past section displays previously held meetings, while the cancelled section lists meetings that have been called off.

<Frame><img src="/images/bookingsTab/image8.jpg" /></Frame>
